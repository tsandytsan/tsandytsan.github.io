Citrus Hack 2020

Citrus Hack is a UC Riverside's premier 24 or 36-hour collegiate-wide hackathon. In the year of 2020, Citrus Hack transitioned to a purely online 24-hour event, The theme was exploring new horizons, inspired by the rising interest and curiousity of space travel (Tesla and Elon Musk was the year's trending topic). For the hackers, the theme was also broadly interpreted to push their projects to their limit and explore that horizon awaiting them.

With the theme in mind, I wanted to approach the overall colors from the bottom up. Generally, when one thinks of space, the general color scheme is dark, with pockets of neon-esque brightness to represent the planets and stars. I wanted to challenge that impression with a light theme and alternatively emphasize the vastness of the universe through dimensions/layers. The marketing committee at the time had people with limited design background so the designs required an approach fit for all skill levels. I found the paper cut and neumorphism styles to fit this mold; as the team can both learn and develop the design fundamentals of shapes, layers, coloring. Paper cut was used for the main design to highlight the main design and style. Neumorphism's minimalist appeal embellished accent designs while maintaining the overall cohesive style.

Cutie Hack 2019

Cutie Hack is a 12-hour beginner-orientated hackathon for students at UC Riverside and high schoolers in the surrounding Riverside area. It is also the sister event for Citrus Hack. For 2019, we went with an ocean theme to highlight concerns for the health of the marine ecosystem. The participants were encouraged to create projects relating to the theme while developing their programming fundamentals.

For an ocean theme, blue is a clear colorway to represent water and its seemingly bottomless depths. For this event, we started with the cover photo and pulled elements from the graphic into the other designs. The cover photo itself features the UC Riverside's bell tower, which is a signature representative of the school. Giving the surrounding fishes a light yellow accent became a repetitive element for the succeeding designs as well, allowing it to be easily distinguished as a theme.
